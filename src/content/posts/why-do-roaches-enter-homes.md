Cockroaches are persistent indoor pests notorious for finding their way into homes through small openings, posing numerous challenges once inside. Discover what attracts cockroaches to residences and effective measures to prevent their intrusion.

Why Cockroaches Enter Homes

1. Food Source

Cockroaches are versatile eaters, consuming a wide range of foods commonly found in homes, from grains and cereals to meats and pet food. To deter infestations, store all dry foods in tightly-sealed plastic containers, clean countertops regularly to eliminate crumbs and spills, and dispose of garbage daily with sealed bins.

2. Water Access

Regular access to water is essential for cockroach survival, leading them to seek moisture-rich areas like kitchens, bathrooms, and laundry rooms. Prevent their entry by promptly fixing leaks and spills, maintaining dry indoor environments, and using dehumidifiers in humid conditions.

3. Warmth

Cockroaches thrive in warm, humid environments, remaining active during summer and seeking shelter indoors as temperatures drop. Seal gaps and cracks around doors, windows, and plumbing fixtures with caulking or weatherstripping to prevent their entry, especially during the cooler seasons.

Preventive Measures

Take proactive steps to deter cockroach infestations:

- Store food in sealed containers
- Clean countertops and floors regularly
- Dispose of garbage promptly and use sealed bins
- Fix plumbing leaks and mop up spills promptly
- Maintain dry indoor environments with dehumidifiers
- Seal entry points with caulking or weatherstripping

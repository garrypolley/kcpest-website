Encountering a mouse in your home is often an unwelcome surprise, signaling potential infestations lurking behind the scenes. With colder weather approaching, mice seek refuge in warm, dry spaces indoors, making it crucial to identify and seal common entry points. As you prepare for the season, take note of these typical spots where mice gain access to homes and safeguard your property accordingly.

Common Entry Points for Mice

1. Dryer Vents

Outdoor dryer vents can inadvertently become gateways for mice seeking shelter. If the vent flaps fail or are damaged, mice easily slip through, drawn by the warmth emitted. Regularly inspect and repair vent flaps and tubes to deter mice from entering your home.

2. HVAC Units

HVAC units, particularly outdoor compressor units, offer mice convenient entry points. Besides damaging wires and lines, mice can infiltrate ductwork, leading to potential indoor infestations. Listen for noises in ducts or notice unusual odors, signs of mice presence, and act swiftly to address the issue.

3. Garages

Warm, food-rich environments like garages are enticing to mice. Ensure garage doors are closed when not in use, and seal all windows and doors to prevent mice from venturing into your home from these accessible areas.

4. Windows and Thresholds

Mice's adeptness at squeezing through tiny openings makes gaps around windows and doors prime entry points. Conduct thorough inspections, repairing seals and closing off any potential access points to thwart rodent intrusions.

Carpenter ants, distinguished by their large size and black or red coloration, are known for tunneling through wood, potentially causing significant structural damage to homes. While they don't pose a direct threat to humans, identifying signs of their presence is crucial to prevent extensive property harm. Apart from observing these ants roaming indoors, here are key indicators that you may be facing a carpenter ant infestation.

How to Identify Carpenter Ant Infestation

1. Presence of Tiny Holes in Woodwork

Carpenter ants carve small holes in wooden surfaces as they excavate tunnels and establish nests. These holes, typically found near doors, windows, trim, and structural elements, are accompanied by piles of sawdust. Since carpenter ants don't consume wood, they push out the debris, leading to noticeable sawdust piles.

2. Audible Rustling Sounds

As carpenter ants burrow through wood, they produce subtle rustling noises. While these sounds may be faint, they can be heard as scraping or movement within walls, ceilings, or beams. Although rustling can indicate various infestations, combining it with observed holes and sawdust suggests a carpenter ant presence.

3. Sighting of Wings

Flying ants, often reproductive members of carpenter ant colonies, are a clear sign of an infestation. Homeowners may mistake them for other flying insects, but their presence around wood features like beams or trim indicates a carpenter ant issue. These flying ants have transparent or light brown wings.
